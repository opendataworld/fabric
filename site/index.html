What is a data fabric?

A data fabric is an architecture that delivers unified, governed access to data spread across many sources — without forcing it all into one place. Instead of point-to-point integrations, a shared semantic layer describes, connects, and governs every asset.

Unified

One canonical model across catalogs, graphs, lakes, and APIs. No more N×N glue between systems.

Governed

Policy and Constraint are first-class — so access, residency, and audit are built in by construction, not bolted on.

Connected

Reified relationships turn isolated assets into a living knowledge graph that agents and people can navigate.

AI-native

Capabilities and Objectives give agents a machine-readable model of what they can do and why.

How Fabric works

Four planes, one model. Each plane is composed from the same foundation primitives.

01

Connect

Ingest or virtualize any source. Every source and dataset becomes a typed asset.

02

Catalog

Describe assets, identities, relationships, and lifecycle state — a self-describing graph.

03

Govern

Attach constraints and policies; every decision and change is an immutable, auditable event.

04

Activate

Search, query, and serve governed data to apps and agents through declared capabilities.
